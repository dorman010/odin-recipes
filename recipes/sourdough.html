Sourdough Bread

A good loaf of sourdough bread is one of the most precies forms of baking, and I've grown to love it over the last couple of years.

I recommend using a sourdough calculator to make sure you measure everything accurately. What you'll need:

Ingredients

  • Strong white bread flour (at least 12% protein): 450g
  • Salt: 10g
  • Water: 300g
  • Sourdough starter: 100g (at 100% hydration)

Steps

  1. Make sure you feed your starter the day before you start this process so it's active and hungry the next day
  2. In a bowl, mix the water with the starter and salt. Gradually add in the flour and mix until it gets fully incorporated.
  3. Let rest in a covered container for 8-9 hours.
  4. Remove dough from container onto a floured surface. Work into shape of basket, leave for 10 minutes to ensure it retains its shape. Afterwards, put into basket and let it proof overnight.
  5. The next morning, pre-heat oven to 230 degrees C. Once the oven is hot, remove the dough from the fridge. Put onto e.g. a cast iron casserole dish, score the bread diagonally, and insert (covered) into the oven for 45 minutes. Rmove cover after 20 minutes.
  6. Oce your bread is done, let it cool on a rack of some sort for an hour before slicing in - resist the temptation to have some immediately, as the bread is still cooking on the inside!
